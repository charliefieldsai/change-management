The Work

Goldman Sachs didn't book a trainer.
They requested a consultant.

"Stay. Learn what our people need. Build it for them."

When I was delivering enterprise onboarding for Marimba's platform in 2000, Goldman Sachs pulled me aside after an initial engagement. They didn't want a standard rollout. They wanted someone who would sit with their leadership, attend their departmental meetings, understand the real gaps — and then go build something exclusively for their organization.

So I did. I spent evenings in my hotel writing curriculum designed around what their managers had told me that day. The result was a change program built from the inside out — not a vendor deliverable, but a genuine organizational transition.

That's the work I do. Not training. Change management consulting.

Ernst & Young trusted that same approach enough to fly me to London to lead their international teams through platform transitions. Twenty years later, the method is the same: listen first, diagnose what the organization actually needs, design for the real barriers, and stay through the change until it holds.
